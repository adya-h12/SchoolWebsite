Achivements

Senate Page Program
Cab Calloway Senior gets into the Senate Page Program!

Cab Calloway senior, Risha Kumar got into the Senate Page Program. The Senate Page Program is administrated by the Sergeant at Arms, the Secretary of the Senate and the party secretaries. Senate Pages play an important role in the day-to-day operations of the Senate. The Senate Page Program allows students a unique opportunity to learn about Congress while continuing with their coursework. Page responsibilities center around their work assisting Senators in the Senate Chamber when Congress is in session.This is an incrdible achivments as the program is very competitive!

TSA National Competion
Cab Calloway's Middle School and High School place at TSA National Competion!

Cab Calloway Middle and High School Students competed in a grueling 5 day competition, at the 41st National Technology Student Association Competition in National Harbor Maryland this past weekend. The Technology Student Association, is a national student organization created to develop skills in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) as well as business education. TSA aims to develop leadership, academic, and business management skills in the workplace among students and leaders within the community. The organization has 250,000 international members. Our students competed in over 20 events throughout the weekend and came away with some national awards!

White House Performance
Cab Calloway's Gospel Choir Performs at the White House!

The Cab Calloway Gospel Choir took part in the famous holiday festivities hosted each year in the White House. The 16-member group, ages 14 through 18, serenaded those touring through the West Wing of the White House with traditional and contemporary gospel songs as well as a few holiday numbers.

Alumnis

Shane van Neerden

Shane van Neerden

Shane van Neerden, Cab Class of 2018 - Since leaving the U.S. in 2018, Shane van Neerden has been developing his classical music performance career with incredible success. When he first began his studies in the Netherlands, he won first prize in a concerto competition as a first-year bachelor student, against three masters students, and consequently played Franz Liszt’s Totentanz with the Conservatorium van Amsterdam Symfonieorkest o.l.v. Ed Spanjaard. He also was chosen to represent the Amsterdam Conservatory in a performance with the students from Moscow’s Gnessin Special School of Music in the Jan Pieterzoon Sweelinck Zaal of the Amsterdam Conservatory. Shane became a finalist in the Ile- de-France International Piano Competition shortly thereafter. In addition to competitions and awards, Shane received a 10 (the highest possible score) for his first year bachelor transition exam. In addition to studying with his own teacher in Amsterdam, Shane has had lessons in the past two years at multiple music festivals: 9de Internationaal Euregio Rijn-Waal Studentenmuziekfestival, studying with Georg Schenck; Schiermonnikoog Jong Talent Festival, in the Netherlands, studying with Jan Wijn; and a public masterclass at the CvA with renowned Italian pianist, Enrico Pace. Shane has also given concerts throughout the Netherlands, Germany, and France. Most recently he appeared on national Dutch television to play a work of one of his favorite composers, Nikolai Medtner. Right before the outbreak of Covid-19, Shane gave a concert in one of the Netherlands most distinguished halls, Muziekgebouw aan ’t IJ, in an all-Beethoven program. Shane also just received a grand piano for his Amsterdam apartment from the National Instrument Funds of the Netherlands.

Sarah McBride

Sarah McBride

Sarah McBride, Class of 2009 - After graduating from Cab, Sarah attended American University and majored in Political Science. While there, Sarah was elected student body president and interned in the Obama White House. After graduating from college, Sarah led the successful effort to pass a landmark non-discrimination bill here in Delaware before going on to work work at the Center for American Progress and as the National Press Secretary for the Human Rights Campaign. In 2016, Sarah made history when she became the first openly transgender person to address a major party convention when she spoke at the Democratic National Convention. Sarah is also the author of the book "Tomorrow Will Be Different," which includes a foreword from Vice President Joe Biden. Sarah has also recently became the Delware's Senator and the first transgender state senator in Jan 2021
